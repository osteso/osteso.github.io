Cosmology

As a researcher in theoretical physics, I worked on early universe cosmology. Gaining a vantage point in this field is an immense challenge: it requires understanding and answering deep questions about the nature and evolution of the universe.
How did our universe begin? Does that question even make sense? How did it evolve to its present state?
The answer to such questions depends on the physics of phenomena where both gravity and quantum physics are important. Given that we do not know the correct theory for the description of these phenomena, researchers are forced to make assumptions with far-reaching consequences. My research consisted in exploring the consequences of models based on the most widely accepted set of assumptions (see wikipedia for more details).
